DENAGO EV NOMAD XL — Electric Vehicle Details

The DENAGO EV NOMAD XL is powered by a 51.2-volt lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) battery rated at 105Ah. This modern battery chemistry delivers a 40-mile range per charge, long cycle life, and stable performance in a wide range of temperatures, and it is backed by a 5-year battery warranty—a major upgrade over the lead-acid packs found in older golf carts.

Drive comes from an efficient 5 kW AC motor paired with a 400-amp Denago controller, delivering smooth, quiet acceleration and a 25 MPH top speed on a 2×4 drivetrain. AC motors run cooler and require less maintenance than older DC systems, which helps the DENAGO EV NOMAD XL stay dependable over years of daily use.

Charging is simple: an on-board lithium quick charger lets you recharge the DENAGO EV NOMAD XL from any standard household outlet—no special equipment or installation required. Combined with zero fuel and minimal maintenance, that keeps day-to-day running costs low.

Is the DENAGO Nomad XL Street Legal?

Yes—the DENAGO EV Nomad XL is a street-legal Medium Speed Vehicle (MSV), also classified as a Low-Speed Vehicle (LSV). With a top speed of 25 MPH and seating for 4, it meets the federal LSV definition under FMVSS No. 500, allowing it to be titled, registered, and driven on many public roads posted at 35 MPH or less (rules vary by state and municipality).

To qualify as street legal, the Nomad XL is equipped with the DOT-required safety equipment for LSVs—headlights, tail and brake lights, turn signals, reflectors, mirrors, a windshield, a parking brake, seat belts, and a 17-digit VIN. Learn more about compliance, state requirements, and how every model measures up on our street legal DENAGO MSV golf cart guide.
