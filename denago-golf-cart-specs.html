Understanding DENAGO Golf Cart Specs

Every DENAGO golf cart shares the same core engineering philosophy: a 51.2-volt lithium drivetrain, a 400-amp Denago controller, an AC motor, and street-legal LSV equipment. The differences between models come down to battery capacity and range, motor output, seating and dimensions, and whether the cart is tuned for on-road comfort or all-terrain use.

Top speed across the lineup is governed by Low-Speed Vehicle rules at 25 MPH, with the City offering a 15–25 MPH range. Range is where the City stands apart at 80 miles per charge—double the 40-mile range of the Nomad and Rover models—thanks to its larger 205Ah battery. Motor power also peaks with the City’s 6.3 kW (12 kW peak) AC motor, while the rest of the lineup uses an efficient 5 kW AC motor. All models charge with an on-board lithium charger, so you can simply plug into a standard outlet.

DENAGO EV City Specs

The DENAGO EV City is the flagship of the lineup and the most fully-featured DENAGO golf cart. It seats 6, travels up to 80 miles per charge, and reaches 15–25 MPH. Power comes from a 51.2V 205Ah LIPO lithium battery paired with a 6.3 kW continuous / 12 kW peak AC motor and a 400-amp Denago controller. It measures 150 × 74 × 78 inches and weighs 1,719 lbs, making it the largest and most substantial cart in the range. Standard equipment includes an NFC key card, a 13-inch display, a secondary screen, a rear camera, a DOT windshield with electric wipers, and a see-through polycarbonate roof. With an on-board intelligent charger and a 2×4 drivetrain, the City is built for premium neighborhood and community driving. MSRP starts at $15,495.

DENAGO EV Nomad Specs

The DENAGO EV Nomad is the compact, value-focused 4-seat model. It offers a 40-mile range, a 25 MPH top speed, and a 51.2V 105Ah LiFePO4 lithium battery driving a 5 kW AC motor through a 400-amp controller. At 114 × 51 × 82 inches and 1,050 lbs, it is the lightest and most maneuverable DENAGO cart, ideal for tight neighborhoods and easy storage. It charges with an on-board lithium quick charger and uses a 2×4 drivetrain. MSRP starts at $8,995.

DENAGO EV Nomad XL Specs

The DENAGO EV Nomad XL shares the Nomad’s drivetrain—40-mile range, 25 MPH top speed, 51.2V 105Ah LiFePO4 battery, and 5 kW AC motor—on an extended platform. The XL frame adds cargo room and a roomier cabin footprint while keeping the same 4-seat capacity and $8,995 starting MSRP. It is the pick for buyers who want Nomad economy with extra space.

DENAGO EV Rover XL Specs

The DENAGO EV Rover XL brings all-terrain capability to the 4-seat format. It pairs a 51.2V 105Ah LiFePO4 lithium battery with a 5 kW AC motor for a 40-mile range and a 25 MPH top speed, and rides on DOT-approved all-terrain tires with lifted suspension. The Rover XL is the entry point into DENAGO’s off-road-capable, street-legal series. MSRP starts at $9,995.

DENAGO EV Rover XL6 Specs

The 2025 DENAGO EV Rover XL6 extends the Rover platform to 6 seats. It keeps the 51.2V 105Ah LiFePO4 battery, 5 kW AC motor, 400-amp controller, and on-board lithium quick charger, with a 40-mile range and 25 MPH top speed. It measures 118 × 49 × 80 inches and weighs 1,202 lbs, and is available in multiple colors including Black. MSRP starts at $11,995.

DENAGO EV Rover XXL Specs

The DENAGO EV Rover XXL is the 6-seat, forward-facing member of the all-terrain Rover family. It uses the same 51.2V 105Ah LiFePO4 lithium battery and 5 kW AC motor as its siblings, with a 25 MPH top speed and a 2×4 drivetrain. Forward-facing seating for six makes it well suited to families and group transport on and off paved roads. MSRP starts at $11,995.

DENAGO Battery & Charging Specs

All six DENAGO golf carts run on a 51.2-volt lithium architecture, but capacity varies. The DENAGO EV City uses the largest pack in the lineup—a 51.2V 205Ah LIPO lithium battery—which is why it delivers up to 80 miles of range per charge. The Nomad, Nomad XL, Rover XL, Rover XL6, and Rover XXL each use a 51.2V 105Ah LiFePO4 lithium battery good for roughly 40 miles per charge. LiFePO4 (lithium iron phosphate) chemistry is prized for its long cycle life, thermal stability, and consistent output. Every model includes an on-board charger—an intelligent charger on the City and a lithium quick charger on the Nomad and Rover models—so you can recharge from a standard outlet without external equipment, and each battery is backed by a 5-year warranty.

DENAGO Motor, Controller & Performance Specs

DENAGO golf carts pair an AC motor with a 400-amp Denago controller for smooth, responsive power delivery. The DENAGO EV City leads with a 6.3 kW continuous / 12 kW peak AC motor, giving it the strongest acceleration and hill-climbing ability in the range—useful given its larger size and six-passenger capacity. The Nomad, Nomad XL, and all three Rover models use an efficient 5 kW AC motor, which balances performance with range on their lighter platforms. Across the board, top speed is capped at 25 MPH to meet Low-Speed Vehicle regulations (the City is rated 15–25 MPH), and every model uses a 2×4 drivetrain. The Rover series adds lifted suspension and DOT-approved all-terrain tires for off-road traction while retaining the same drivetrain fundamentals.

DENAGO Dimensions, Weight & Capacity Specs

Size and weight scale with capability. The DENAGO EV City is the largest cart at 150 × 74 × 78 inches and 1,719 lbs, reflecting its six-seat luxury build. The Nomad and Nomad XL share a compact 114 × 51 × 82-inch footprint at 1,050 lbs—the lightest and most maneuverable carts in the lineup—though the XL uses an extended frame for additional cargo room. The six-seat Rover XL6 measures 118 × 49 × 80 inches and weighs 1,202 lbs. Seating ranges from four passengers (Nomad, Nomad XL, Rover XL) to six passengers (City, Rover XL6, Rover XXL), so you can match the cart to your typical passenger and cargo load.

DENAGO Golf Cart Specs FAQ

How fast is a DENAGO golf cart?

Every DENAGO golf cart has a top speed of 25 MPH (the City is rated 15–25 MPH), consistent with Low-Speed Vehicle regulations.

What is the range of a DENAGO golf cart?

Range is about 40 miles per charge on the Nomad, Nomad XL, and Rover models, and up to 80 miles on the DENAGO EV City thanks to its larger 205Ah battery.

What battery do DENAGO golf carts use?

DENAGO carts use 51.2-volt lithium batteries—a 205Ah LIPO pack on the City and 105Ah LiFePO4 packs on the Nomad and Rover models—each covered by a 5-year warranty.

Which DENAGO Golf Cart Is Right for You?

Use the specs above to match a model to your needs. Choose the City if range and premium features matter most—its 80-mile range and 6.3 kW motor lead the lineup. Choose the Nomad or Nomad XL for the most affordable 4-seat option, with the XL adding cargo space. Choose a Rover XL, Rover XL6, or Rover XXL if you want all-terrain capability, stepping up in seating from four to six as you move through the range.
